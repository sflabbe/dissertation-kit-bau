\section{Mathematical Derivations}
\label{sec:math_derivations}

\subsection{Derivation of Equations of Motion}
\label{subsec:derivation_eom}

Starting from Newton's second law for the coupled system:

\begin{equation}
	F_1 = m_1\ddot{x}_1
	\label{eq:newton1}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
	F_2 = m_2\ddot{x}_2
	\label{eq:newton2}
\end{equation}

The coupling forces are:
\begin{equation}
	F_c = k(x_1 - x_2) + c(\dot{x}_1 - \dot{x}_2)
	\label{eq:coupling_force}
\end{equation}

Combining yields the system equations presented in Chapter~\ref{ch:modeling}.

\section{Numerical Methods}
\label{sec:numerical_methods}

\subsection{Newmark Integration}
\label{subsec:newmark}

The Newmark-$\beta$ method uses update equations:

\begin{equation}
	\mathbf{x}_{n+1} = \mathbf{x}_n + \Delta t\dot{\mathbf{x}}_n + \frac{\Delta t^2}{2}[(1-2\beta)\ddot{\mathbf{x}}_n + 2\beta\ddot{\mathbf{x}}_{n+1}]
	\label{eq:newmark_disp}
\end{equation}

For stability, $\beta = 0.25$ is typically used.

\subsection{Time Step Selection}
\label{subsec:timestep}

The time step is selected based on:
\begin{equation}
	\Delta t \leq \frac{T_{\min}}{20}
	\label{eq:timestep_criterion}
\end{equation}

where $T_{\min}$ is the shortest period of interest.

\section{Additional Results}
\label{sec:additional_results}

\subsection{Convergence Studies}
\label{subsec:convergence}

Convergence was verified by varying time step and comparing results. Results converged for $\Delta t < 0.001$~s.

\subsection{Sensitivity Analysis}
\label{subsec:sensitivity}

Sensitivity to parameter variations was investigated:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Mass: $\pm 10\%$ variation causes $\pm 5\%$ response change
	\item Stiffness: $\pm 10\%$ variation causes $\pm 8\%$ response change
	\item Damping: $\pm 10\%$ variation causes $\pm 3\%$ response change
\end{itemize}

\section{Software Implementation}
\label{sec:software}

The model is implemented in MATLAB/Python. Main components:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \texttt{system\_model.m}: System equations
	\item \texttt{integrator.m}: Time integration
	\item \texttt{postprocess.m}: Results analysis
\end{itemize}

Typical computation time: less than 10 seconds for 1000 time steps.
